Platform Launch: 9 Months to Production

Product Case Study ‚Ä¢ Hedfirst

The Challenge: Ship Multi-Tenant Platform in 9 Months

Co-led product strategy for multi-tenant telehealth platform build from concept to production. The goal: launch with real paying patients, live prescriptions, and operational pharmacy fulfillment‚Äînot a prototype.

Product Lifecycle

Phase 1: Vision & Strategy (Ideation)

After leaving CompoundLive/Rugiet (acquired by ListenCo), I proposed building a next-generation telehealth platform incorporating everything I'd learned. The vision:

  • Multi-tenant SaaS architecture supporting multiple healthcare brands
  • Native AI capabilities for clinical documentation
  • Better clinical workflows learned from previous platforms
  • Flexible product configuration and billing models
  • HIPAA compliance and operational excellence from day one

Phase 2: User Research & Validation

Conducted stakeholder research and user interviews with chronic migraine patients to validate business model:

  • Validated $499/year membership model with target patients
  • Interviewed doctors about clinical workflow needs
  • Researched competitor platforms to identify gaps
  • Analyzed pharmacy partner capabilities and state coverage
  • Evaluated regulatory requirements across 48 states

Phase 3: Prioritization & Roadmap (Implementation)

Product judgment: Prioritized pharmacy integration and e-prescribing (must-have for launch) while cutting admin analytics and reporting features (nice-to-have, could add post-launch).

Critical tradeoff decisions:

Must-Have for Launch

  • Patient portal with account management
  • Doctor portal with consultation workflows
  • E-prescribing via DoseSpot integration
  • Pharmacy fulfillment with routing logic
  • Payment processing via PayTheory
  • Multi-tenant architecture with data isolation
  • HIPAA-compliant infrastructure

Post-Launch Features

  • Advanced analytics and reporting dashboards
  • Marketing automation and email campaigns
  • Detailed financial reporting
  • A/B testing infrastructure
  • Mobile applications

Phase 4: Execution & Delivery

Strategic thinking: Coordinated 4 engineers + CTO through 12 two-week sprints with 95% on-time delivery.

  • Sprint planning with clear priorities and acceptance criteria
  • Daily standups to unblock engineering
  • Bi-weekly demos to stakeholders for feedback
  • Continuous prioritization adjustments based on learnings
  • Technical spec writing for complex integrations

Made critical vendor decision: chose Choose Health over competitors for lab integration due to better API documentation, even though it was more expensive‚Äîthis decision saved 2+ weeks of integration time.

Phase 5: Beta Testing & Iteration

Soft launch with beta patients before full marketing push:

  • Recruited 25 beta patients for initial cohort
  • Monitored every prescription, consultation, and payment
  • Gathered feedback on user experience and clinical workflows
  • Fixed bugs and refined features based on real usage
  • Validated operational processes with pharmacy partners

Phase 6: Launch & Scale

Official launch: August 4, 2025, with real paying patients rather than perfect features.

Key Lessons

Ruthless prioritization enabled aggressive timeline. By cutting nice-to-have features (analytics, marketing automation) and focusing on core patient/doctor workflows, we launched 3 months earlier. Shipping with real paying patients validated product-market fit and operational readiness better than any internal testing could. The 95% on-time delivery rate came from clear priorities, detailed specs, and daily communication.
